Egg Documentation

Egg on Tempo, explained.

Egg uses one capped supply that can live in two forms: Egg NFTs and $EGG. These docs explain minting, hatching, trading, and the rules that keep the combined total fixed at 13,200 units.

Overview

Egg combines collectible inventory and token liquidity inside one fixed supply. Users mint Egg NFTs at a fixed price, then decide whether to keep them as unhatched inventory or burn them to hatch liquid $EGG into circulation.

The core idea is simple: Egg NFTs and $EGG are two forms of the same capped supply. The ratio can change over time, but the combined total does not.

Unhatched Egg NFTs + Circulating $EGG = 13,200
The combined total remains fixed.

Asset Model

Egg has two states for the same underlying unit:

  • An Egg NFT represents an unhatched unit still living on the NFT side.
  • A $EGG token represents a hatched unit now living on the liquid token side.

A unit cannot exist in both states at once. When an Egg NFT is burned, that specific NFT-backed unit leaves the NFT side and appears as one unit of $EGG.

Before hatch

The user holds an Egg NFT. That unit is part of the unhatched inventory.

After hatch

The NFT is destroyed and the user receives one unit of liquid token supply.

System result

The total stays fixed while the ratio between the two forms changes.

Mint

Minting is how supply first enters the NFT side. Each mint costs 0.3 pathUSD and creates exactly one Egg NFT.

Mint rules

  • Egg has a hard cap of 13,200 total units.
  • Each mint consumes one unit from the remaining unminted supply.
  • Minting gives an Egg NFT, not liquid $EGG.
  • Minted NFTs can later be burned to hatch tokens at a 1:1 rate.

What minting means

Minting gives an Egg NFT first. $EGG only appears after a burn. The important things to check before minting are the price, quantity, and remaining supply.

Burn to Hatch

Burning is the core mechanic of Egg. An Egg NFT can be destroyed in exchange for 1 $EGG. This action is intentionally irreversible.

Burn rules

  • Burning one Egg NFT yields one unit of $EGG.
  • The NFT is removed from circulation permanently.
  • The hatched unit appears on the token side of the supply.
  • The burn cannot be reversed after confirmation.

Before you hatch

Burning is permanent. Once the transaction is confirmed, the NFT is gone and the supply mix updates immediately. Check the selected NFTs and expected output carefully before signing.

Trade

Trading exists for the hatched token supply. As more Eggs hatch, more $EGG enters circulation and market depth can grow.

What to watch

  • Current price and 24-hour change for $EGG.
  • Volume, liquidity, and recent trade flow.
  • Circulating supply of hatched $EGG.
  • How much supply is still unhatched on the NFT side.

How trading fits Egg

Trading only begins after supply hatches. That makes market activity part of the same story as minting and burning, not a separate system.

Supply Logic

The key to Egg is that the combined total is fixed while the visible market composition changes over time.

Example

  • If 13,200 Egg NFTs exist and none are burned, circulating $EGG is 0.
  • If 2,000 Egg NFTs are burned, 11,200 Egg NFTs remain and 2,000 $EGG circulate.
  • If 5,284 Egg NFTs are burned, 7,916 Egg NFTs remain and 5,284 $EGG circulate.

This makes it easy to see how much supply remains dormant, how much has hatched, and how the market evolves as users make irreversible choices.

FAQ

Is the total supply fixed?

Yes. The combined total of unhatched Egg NFTs and circulating $EGG remains capped at 13,200 units.

What do users receive at mint?

Users receive an Egg NFT. They do not receive liquid $EGG at mint time.

What happens when a user burns an NFT?

The Egg NFT is destroyed and the user receives exactly one unit of $EGG.

Can the burn be reversed?

No. Burning is one-way by design.

Where will contract addresses be listed?

Once live, contract addresses can be published in the docs and on the site so they are easy to verify.
